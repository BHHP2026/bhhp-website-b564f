America’s Most
Beloved Island

Hilton Head Island sits at the southern tip of South Carolina, connected to the mainland by a single bridge that has kept it beautifully insulated from overdevelopment. What you find here is a rare thing: natural beauty preserved at scale, with a sophisticated infrastructure layered gently over it.

The island spans roughly 42 square miles, almost a third of which is protected natural land. Building height restrictions, underground utilities, and meticulous land-use planning have kept Hilton Head looking almost exactly as its founders envisioned — a destination where the environment comes first.

That philosophy has paid off. Travel + Leisure readers have consistently voted Hilton Head Island among the best islands in the United States — a recognition that draws thousands of visitors every year and a growing cohort of buyers who decide, mid-vacation, that they never want to leave.

The Hilton Head Lifestyle
Everything You Want.
Nothing You Don’t.
01

World-Class Golf

Twenty-four championship courses — including Harbour Town Golf Links, host of the annual PGA Tour’s RBC Heritage — make Hilton Head one of the premier golf destinations on the planet.

02

Pristine Beaches

Twelve miles of wide, hard-packed Atlantic beach — one of the most family-friendly shorelines on the East Coast, with calm surf, warm water, and miles of unspoiled natural scenery.

03

World-Class Tennis

Hilton Head has earned a global reputation as a tennis destination, with more courts per capita than virtually any comparably sized community in America.

04

Dining & Culture

From celebrated chef-driven restaurants to waterfront tiki bars, the island’s dining scene punches well above its weight. The Arts Center of Coastal Carolina brings Broadway-caliber performances year-round.

05

Water Sports & Marina Life

Kayaking, paddleboarding, deep-sea fishing, dolphin cruises, and sailing — with the Atlantic on one side and the Intracoastal Waterway on the other, water activities are a way of life here.

06

Nature & Wildlife

Packs of bottle-nosed dolphins, loggerhead sea turtles nesting on the beach, great blue herons in the salt marshes — Hilton Head’s natural world is an ever-present reminder of what makes this place extraordinary.
