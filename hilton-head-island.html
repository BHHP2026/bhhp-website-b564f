America's Favorite Island®

Every Moment Shaped
by Nature

Dolphins in the surf, turtles on the shore, families on bikes, and golf where marsh meets sky — every moment on Hilton Head Island is shaped by the Lowcountry's natural beauty. This 12-mile barrier island off the South Carolina coast was designed from its founding to live in harmony with its breathtaking natural surroundings: ancient live oaks, tidal marshes, pristine beaches, and the warm waters of the Atlantic.

Today, Hilton Head is home to 26 championship golf courses, 12 miles of wide, flat Atlantic beach, more than 350 tennis courts, 70 miles of paved bike paths, and some of the most sought-after residential communities on the East Coast. Its no-billboard aesthetic, carefully planned greenways, and conservation-first ethos have preserved a lifestyle genuinely unlike anywhere else.

Your Questions, Answered

Frequently Asked
Questions

Everything you need to know about Hilton Head Island — the lifestyle, the real estate, and the island communities that define life here.

Where is Hilton Head Island?
Hilton Head Island is a barrier island off the coast of South Carolina's Lowcountry, approximately 95 miles north of Savannah, Georgia and 40 miles south of Beaufort, SC. The island is roughly 12 miles long and 5 miles wide, accessible via the US-278 causeway. Its position — sheltered by the larger islands of the Sea Islands chain — gives it unusually calm, warm waters and one of the most beautiful stretches of Atlantic shoreline on the East Coast.
How many golf courses are on Hilton Head Island?
Hilton Head Island is home to approximately 26 championship golf courses, earning it the well-deserved nickname "Golf Island." The island's marquee layout is Harbour Town Golf Links in Sea Pines Resort — designed by Pete Dye with Jack Nicklaus and ranked consistently among America's top 100 courses. It hosts the PGA Tour RBC Heritage each April. Other celebrated courses include the three layouts at Palmetto Dunes (Robert Trent Jones, George Fazio, and Arthur Hills), Port Royal's three courses, Palmetto Hall, and Wexford Golf Club.
What are the best communities to buy a home on Hilton Head Island?
Hilton Head's premier residential communities each offer a distinct lifestyle: Sea Pines Resort is the island's crown jewel — a 5,000-acre gated community with Harbour Town Golf Links, the RBC Heritage, a Forbes Four-Star hotel, private beach, and the iconic Harbour Town Lighthouse. Palmetto Dunes offers oceanfront resort living with 3 miles of beach and 25 miles of lagoons. Port Royal Plantation is entirely private and oceanfront, where Port Royal Sound meets the Atlantic. Hilton Head Plantation is the island's largest community — 4,000 acres with four golf courses and a true neighbourhood character. Wexford Plantation is the most exclusive, with a private inland harbour and deep-water boat slips available only to residents.
What is the Hilton Head Island real estate market like?
Hilton Head's real estate market is among the most resilient on the East Coast. With no new developable land, a conservation-protected island footprint, sustained demand from retirees and second-home buyers, and a robust short-term rental market, island properties have demonstrated consistent appreciation over time. The island-wide median price is approximately $850,000, though Sea Pines averages closer to $1.85 million and oceanfront estates regularly trade above $5 million. BHHP can provide detailed community-specific market analysis tailored to your investment goals.
What is the RBC Heritage golf tournament?
The RBC Heritage presented by Boeing is an annual PGA Tour event held each April at Harbour Town Golf Links within Sea Pines Resort — traditionally the week after The Masters. It is one of the most scenic and beloved events on Tour, with the candy-stripe Harbour Town Lighthouse forming the backdrop for the dramatic par-4 18th hole finishing beside Calibogue Sound. RBC Heritage week is the island's most festive and sought-after week of the year, and properties in Sea Pines command significant premium rental rates during this period.
Is Hilton Head Island a good place to invest in real estate?
Hilton Head Island consistently ranks among the strongest real estate investment markets on the East Coast, and for compelling reasons: the island's land supply is permanently constrained by conservation easements and geography; demand from retirees, second-home buyers, and vacation rental investors is structural and growing; the island's world-class amenities (golf, beach, tennis, marina, dining) support both long-term appreciation and strong short-term rental yields. Properties in top communities like Sea Pines can generate $30,000–$100,000+ annually in vacation rental income during peak season. Contact BHHP for a tailored investment analysis.
What are the best restaurants on Hilton Head Island?
Hilton Head's culinary scene is exceptional for an island of its size. Must-visit dining experiences include The Salty Dog Cafe at South Beach Marina in Sea Pines (a true island institution for casual waterfront seafood); Hudson's Seafood House on the Docks (a Hilton Head legend since 1967, sourcing 90% of seafood locally from Port Royal Sound); Skull Creek Boathouse (spectacular waterfront sunsets and live music); One Hot Mama's (multiple award-winning BBQ on Main Street); Lucky Rooster Kitchen + Bar (the island's best farm-to-table); and Poseidon at Shelter Cove Harbour (upscale waterfront fine dining). Sea Pines also offers The Quarterdeck rooftop oyster bar at Harbour Town — the island's finest post-round destination.
