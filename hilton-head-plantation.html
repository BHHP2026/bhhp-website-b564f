Hilton Head's Grand Estate Community

Spanning more than 4,000 acres along Hilton Head Island's northern reaches — where the island meets Port Royal Sound — Hilton Head Plantation stands as the island's largest and most storied private gated community. With four championship golf courses, a deep-water marina capable of accommodating vessels to 200 feet, and two protected nature conservancies, it offers a breadth of lifestyle and landscape that no other community on the island can match.

"Hilton Head Plantation is where the island's history, natural grandeur, and championship amenities converge — a community of rare scale and even rarer beauty."

A Plantation Lifestyle

Four championship golf courses, a world-class deep-water marina on Skull Creek, protected nature conservancies, and 4,000 acres of Lowcountry beauty — Hilton Head Plantation offers the island's most complete residential lifestyle.

Four World-Class Courses

The Country Club of Hilton Head Island — a masterpiece by Rees Jones — anchors four championship courses within the plantation. Bear Creek, Oyster Reef, and Dolphin Head complete one of the most compelling multi-course offerings in the entire Southeast.

Whooping Crane Pond Conservancy and the ancient Baynard Ruins — a pre-Civil War tabby plantation complex — anchor two irreplaceable nature preserves within the community, home to wood storks, painted buntings, and native wildlife.

The Plantation Difference

Scale That Stands Alone

No community on Hilton Head Island offers the sheer breadth of Hilton Head Plantation — where 4,000 acres of protected Lowcountry landscape encompass four golf courses, a deep-water marina, private ocean beach, ancient ruins, and two wildlife conservancies in a single private enclave.

  • Country Club of Hilton Head Island — Rees Jones-designed championship course with full club facilities
  • Skull Creek Marina: 158 deep-water slips, vessels to 200 ft, direct ICW access to Port Royal Sound
  • Two miles of private Atlantic beach — exclusively for plantation residents
  • Whooping Crane Pond Conservancy — a protected freshwater ecosystem rich in shorebirds
  • Baynard Ruins — 1790s tabby plantation complex, a protected national historic landmark
  • Old Fort Pub: waterfront dining with legendary sunset views over Skull Creek
  • Over 7 miles of dedicated walking and biking trails throughout the community
