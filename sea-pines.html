What golf courses are in Sea Pines Resort?
Sea Pines Resort is home to four championship golf courses: Harbour Town Golf Links (designed by Pete Dye with Jack Nicklaus, host of the PGA TOUR's RBC Heritage presented by Boeing), Heron Point by Pete Dye, Atlantic Dunes by Davis Love III, and the private Sea Pines Country Club. Harbour Town is consistently ranked among America's top 100 courses and is famous for its lighthouse backdrop at the 18th hole along Calibogue Sound. Justin Thomas won the 2025 RBC Heritage.
How many tennis and pickleball courts are at Sea Pines Racquet Club?
The Sea Pines Racquet Club features 20 Har-Tru clay tennis courts and 6 dedicated pickleball courts, making it one of the finest racquet sports facilities in the Southeast. The club offers USPTA and PTR-certified professional instruction, daily clinics, round-robin events for $10 per person, and complimentary court time for resort guests.
Is Sea Pines a gated community on Hilton Head Island?
Yes. Sea Pines Resort is a private, gated community spanning approximately 5,000 private acres at the southern tip of Hilton Head Island, South Carolina. Founded in 1956 by Charles Fraser, it was one of the nation's first environmentally planned resort communities. Access is controlled at the Sea Pines security gate, and the community maintains its own roads, security, and services year-round.

How tall is the Harbour Town Lighthouse and where is it located?
The Harbour Town Lighthouse stands 90 feet tall above Calibogue Sound and is located within Sea Pines Resort at the southern tip of Hilton Head Island, SC. Open since 1970, the iconic red-and-white striped lighthouse features 114 steps visitors can climb for panoramic views of the Sound, ocean, and coastal marshland. The lighthouse is the centerpiece of Harbour Town Marina village and the instantly recognizable backdrop for the RBC Heritage's famous finishing hole.
